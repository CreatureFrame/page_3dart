pigeon panic
pigeon rigs for AR mobile game



Contract 3D art and animation work for Combo Studio on Pigeon Panic, an augmented reality mobile game for iOS in which the player chases virtual pigeons.

For this job it was important to ensure the finished assets were not only mobile-ready, but further optimised for the added demands of an AR game, running with dozens of instances of the pigeons on the screen at once.

Additionally, the client was interested in having squash and stretch integrated into the rig, and all the final animations were to be shared between the three different shaped characters.

My solution to increase efficiency in the assets was to limit the number of influences per vertex during the skinning process, in addition to sculpting corrective blend shapes over the bone animations to smooth out the result in the wings, reducing the amount of calculation required at runtime.

The squash and stretch system built into the pigeons necks succesfully brought scaling animation data into Unity effectively and further saved time during the animating process.


For the sake of efficiency, the pigeons were set up to account for being able to share animations between them.

The animations were further manually tweaked where needed to suit their various body shapes and personalities.


The assets were made in Maya and I worked from concept art provided by the client. They were delivered as Unity package files with the animations set up and ready for import into the project.
